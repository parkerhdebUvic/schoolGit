Computer Science Labs at UVic

Labs help students grow their skills introductory computer science courses

1Simply reading, watching and listening can teach us only so much. Whereas learning by doing is active learning, and very different from learning by passively consuming. We may consume all the information available, but to truly learn a new technology, we must actually use it and put our skills to the test. In the case of learning programming, we must write code.

At the start of each slide set, I post links to good online resources that have exercises you can work through to practice the concepts covered in lecture. Two good resources are:

  • Codecademy
  • W3Schools

In CSC 130, the labs are designed to provide you with an opportunity to practice important concepts introduced during lecture.

Use the worksheets from lecture as a reference when working through the lab work.

The worksheets and lab work will be your main resources for studying for the two midterms. The worksheets and lab activities should help you with some parts of your term projects as well.

CSC 130 labs are held in the ECS building
